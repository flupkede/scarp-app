Two ways to pitch, depending on the format. Both use verified facts (see docs/PROJECT_RATIONALE.md).

## OPTION A — Walk through the live Story page (recommended)

Open `scarp.dsoft.services/story` and scroll slide by slide. The page IS your visual aid — talking over a real, working product beats static slides. ~90s at a calm pace; stretch or trim per slide.

**Slide 6 — Credits / close**
> "Open source, public data, MIT licensed. And the same method works for the Alps, the Himalayas — anywhere a warming world is turning mountains into hazards. That's Scarp."

## OPTION B — Standalone 60-second pitch (no screen)

**[0–12s] Hook**
> "In 1958, a landslide in Lituya Bay, Alaska, made the highest wave ever recorded — 524 metres. Last August, Tracy Arm made the second-highest — 481 — in a fjord full of cruise ships, at half past five in the morning. Nobody saw it coming."

**[12–25s] Problem**
> "These slides are getting more common as glaciers retreat. Detection exists — but nobody is systematically deciding *where* to watch next. Geologist Hig Higman builds his own sensors and installs them by hand, alone. He has no map telling him where the next one matters most."

**[25–45s] Demo / what it does**
> "So I built Scarp. It ranks every part of Southeast Alaska on susceptibility, the fjord-wall signal, exposure, and the monitoring gap. Every score is explainable — click a site, see exactly why it ranks. And it's honest about its blind spots: the grey shows where public data is too thin to judge — three-quarters of the region."

**[45–60s] Close**
> "Barry Arm — the famous one — ranks low on purpose, because it's already monitored. Scarp doesn't find dangerous places. It finds places that are dangerous *and* unwatched. Open source, public data, and the method works anywhere a warming world is turning mountains into hazards."

---

## Delivery notes (both options)

- Two lines that must land, with a pause after each: **"Nobody saw it coming"** and **"dangerous *and* unwatched."**
- The before/after slider and the confidence-layer toggle are your two strongest LIVE moments — use them; they prove it's real.
- If asked "why isn't Lituya Bay in your top 10?": *"Lituya is well-covered in our data — it scores, it's just not top-ranked. The point isn't the famous sites; it's the unwatched slopes between them."*
- If asked "did you predict Tracy Arm?": *"No — and I'm careful not to claim that. Tracy Arm ranks 61st because the 2025 event isn't in any public inventory yet. That's the data lag the tool exists to expose."*
- Don't oversell. The honesty (data-limited zones, "not a validated model") is *why* it's credible.
- Verify before you present: that Barry Arm shows the "already monitored" note, the confidence toggle works, and the search returns something sensible.
